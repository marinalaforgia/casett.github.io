Outreach

Undergraduate Mentoring

I believe that undergraduate mentoring is an invaluable opportunity to help support undergraduate interns experience science, as well as to help them explore possible future careers and further their education. I have been privileged throughout my life to have wonderful mentors and research experiences and I am now committed to providing a safe, supportive environment for my undergraduates to explore the scientific world. Undergraduates from all backgrounds are welcome to contact me about research opportunities! Current undergraduate projects involve: culturing and identifying the fungi associated with seagrasses, fungal microscopy and some fieldwork to collect seagrass from beds in Bodega Bay, CA.

Science Communication

I am passionate about science communication. Our article in Frontiers for Young Minds, "Even Superheroes Need Help Sometimes: Three Incredible Tales of Microbial Symbiosis", discusses the different types of symbiosis using three microbe-animal systems. I was also involved in the development of a microbiome board game, Gut Check.

Expanding Your Horizons (EYH)

EYH conferences introduce 5th-8th grade girls to STEM careers. Since 2014, I have co-lead the "Genome Detectives" workshop at UC Berkeley.
